Research Experiences

  • B.Sc. Thesis, Electrical Engineering Department, Analytical Electrodynamic Modelling Of Dielectric Barrier Discharge Plasma Actuators With Considering The Metamaterial Properties Of The Plasmonic Region

    Supervisor: Dr. AmirNader Askarpour

    The purpose is to provide a mathematical model to analytically simulate plasma-based flow control actuators based on the electrodynamic considerations of the plasmonic medium. The objective is to develop the model based on fewer assumptions but more flexible for controlling purposes. My ultimate goal is to engineer the dielectric to make it tunable to specific modes to control the magnitude and the direction of the momentum vector produced by the plasma actuator.

  • B.Sc. Thesis, Aerospace Engineering Department, Computational Modelling Of Dielectric Barrier Discharge Plasma Actuators With Considering The Metamaterial Properties Of The Plasma

    Supervisor: Dr. Sahar Noori
    Mentor: Mr. Gholamreza Abdizadeh

    The purpose was to provide a computational model for the DBD plasma actuators, including the plasma's metamaterial properties. The model solved the governing Poisson's Equation on the considered domain, treating the plasmonic region as a dielectric media with a calculated permittivity representing the plasmonic region under excitation.

  • Investigated The Aerodynamic Characteristics Of NACA 0012, NACA 23012, NACA 4412 And SELIG 1223 Airfoils

    Supervisor: Dr. M.Tadjfar
    Mentor: Mr. Amin Jaberi

    The purpose was to provide This was thorough comparison between the numerical data from Xfoil and the experimental data from the references, regarding the aerodynamic characteristics of NACA 0012, NACA 23012, NACA 4412 and SELIG 1223 airfoils.

  • Investigated The Various Numerical Solutions And Estimation Methods For Solving Blasius Equation

    Supervisor: Dr. M.Tadjfar
    Mentor: Mr. Amin Jaberi

    The purpose was to investigate the various numerical methods that are used to solve or estimate the results of the Blasius equation that represents the case of a laminar zero pressure gradient boundary layer flow on a flat plate.

Computational Analyses

  • Implemented the analysis method of arbitrarily oriented microstrip transmission lines in arbitrarily shaped dielectric media on the structure of a DBD plasma actuator, MATLAB, Fall 2020

    My purpose was to provide a calculation to obtain the charge densities on the interfaces of a DBD actuator setup, solving one of the two parts of calculating the body force which is produced by the actuator.

  • Implemented Complex Integration Methods and Gaussian Quadratures for the highly oscillatory function of interest, MATLAB, Mathematica, Fall 2020

    My purpose was to numerically find the Inverse Hankel Transform of an electric potential function, representing the Green's Function for a source-embedded two-layered medium with known boundary conditions.

  • Analyzed two cone models with different shape angles and a spherical model, 3D Domain, Supersonic Flow Regime, Comsol Multi-physics, Spring 2020

    The project was to simulate the shock patterns around defined models to explore their 3D physics. Then we used the Schlieren photography method to validate the results of the simulation.

  • Flow Simulation over an RAE airfoil, Transonic Flow Regime, Ansys Fluent, Spring 2020

    The project was to simulate the flow of the RAE2822 airfoil, which has a delta shock within the transonic regime. The position and the power of the shock were captured correctly. I ran the simulation using three methods of k-omega SST, Spalart Almaras, and Transition SST. I got the best result from the latter one for more computation cost. The analysis consisted domain and mesh study, as well.

  • Analyzed the heat transfer of a laptop chip-set, MATLAB, Spring 2019

    The project was to analyze the heat transfer when having an energy source and defined boundary conditions. I First, simulated the temperature distribution among the laptop chip-set steadily and then extended the simulation to capture its variations in time.

Designs and Simulations

  • Designed an Earth-to-Mars trip based on Hohmann transfer, STK, MATLAB, Spring 2021

    The trip starts from the parking orbit around the Earth and calculated using MATLAB at reasonable launch date. The trip has three main phases: 1.Escaping from the Earth orbit, 2.going through the Hohmann transfer orbit, 3.entering the Mars's orbit, and the controlling parameter was the Thrust vector of the probe.

  • Simulated the dynamics and the vibration pattern of a MEMS sensor, MATLAB Simulink, Fall 2018

    The purpose was to first analyze a MEMS system analytically, regarding to its dynamics and vibrational patterns, and second, to simulate the characteristics of the system based on the governing equations using MATLAB Simulink.

  • Designed a controller for an inverted pendulum, MATLAB Simulink, Fall 2018

    The controller was designed based on different strategies, Compensation, Lead-Lag, PD, PI, and PID in order to find the best senario, analytically in the first step and then to simulate the design using MATLAB Simulink.

  • Designed a 3-degree-freedom simulator, Airbus-A320, MATLAB Simulink, Fall 2018

    The purpose was to first, design an aircraft simulator by implementing the governing differential equations in Simulink and then use it to evaluate two of the widely known flight maneuvers, the cruise and the glide phases.
