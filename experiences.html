Research Experiences

  • Topological Data Analysis of Dark Matter Density Field in the N-body Simulations

    • Duration: 10/2021 - 07/2022
    • Type of Research: Computational, Data Science, Theoretical
    • Institution or Organization: Complex Systems Laboratory, Shahid Beheshti University
    • Supervisor: Dr. Mohammad Sadegh Movahed
    Description:

    I collaborate with a team of 4 to study the changing topological structure of the cosmic web using the tools of topological data analysis. We have implemented the Persistent Homology technique to examine the topology of large-scale structure at zero redshift within the cosmological standard model framework and interacting dark energy-dark matter models. We are currently conducting an analysis of the redshift evolution of large-scale structure with the use of persistence diagrams and Betti numbers from redshifts z=46.8 to z=0.

  • Electrodynamic Analysis of Dielectric Barrier Discharge (DBD) Actuators Based on The Properties of Low-Frequency Plasmons

    • Duration: 05/2020 - 09/2021
    • Type of Research: Computational, Theoretical
    • Institution or Organization: Compressible Flow Laboratory, Amirkabir University of Technology
    • Supervisor: Dr. Amirnader Askarpour
    Description:

    This research was for my thesis for B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, Electromagnetics I conceptualized the DBD actuators as a two-layered medium and implemented the properties of low-frequency plasmons based on the Lorentz Model to simulate the plasma layer of the actuator’s structure. I then numerically discretized the integral equation relating the electric potential to the charge density on the actuator’s electrodes based on the Method of Moments. I determined the closed-form spatial Green’s Function based on the Complex Images Method for electrostatic fields and the Generalized pencil-of-function numerical algorithm. Finally, I implemented Finite Difference Approximation to post-process the fields for validation purposes.

  • Computational Modelling of Dielectric Barrier Discharge (DBD) Actuators Based on The Properties of Low Frequency Plasmons

    • Duration: 05/2020 - 04/2021
    • Type of Research: Computational, Experimental, Theoretical
    • Institution or Organization: Compressible Flow Laboratory, Amirkabir University of Technology
    • Supervisor: Dr. Sahar Noori
    • Mentor: Dr. Gholamreza Abdizadeh
    Description:

    This research was for my thesis for B.Sc. in Aerospace Engineering, Aerodynamics I studied the dominant models for the simulation of DBD actuators. I further developed a computational model by implementing the properties of low-frequency plasmons based on the Lorentz Model to incorporate the plasma presence. I formulated the energy transfer from the plasmonic region to the ambient flow in terms of body force components incorporated into the Navier Stokes equations as the source terms. Finally, I experimented with the plasma actuation with the metric of maximum induced velocity proportional to the total body force and validated the performance of the presented model.

Computational Analyses

  • Electrostatic Field Computation of a Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuator | MATLAB, Mathematica | Fall 2020

    I polished and programmed the method of analyzing arbitrarily oriented microstrip transmission lines in arbitrarily-shaped dielectric media. Furthermore, I computed the electrostatic fields and the capacitance matrix for the structure of a dielectric barrier discharge actuator based on the programmed method.

  • Derivation of The Closed-Form Electrostatic Green's Function for The Structure of a Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuator | MATLAB, Mathematica | Fall 2020

    I implemented the numerical method for evaluating the highly oscillatory Bessel transform based on rewriting the Bessel function in terms of the WhittakerW function. Based on the method, I rewrote the Bessel transform as the sum of two integrals and then transformed one into the Fourier type, and computed by the generalized Gauss–Laguerre quadrature and logarithmic Gauss–Laguerre quadrature; furthermore, I computed the other integral based on the evaluation of special functions.

  • Three Dimensional Analysis of the Shock Patterns and Flow Interactions With Aerodynamic Shapes | COMSOL Multiphysics | Spring 2020

    I simulated the supersonic flow over two cone models with different shape angles and a spherical model in the 3D Domain to explore the flow field before and after the shock waves. I Collaborated with a team of 5 to conduct a visualization experiment using the Schlieren photography method to validate the numerical simulation results.

    Part of the Aerodynamics II Laboratory course.

  • Numerical Analysis of The Flow over an RAE Airfoil in Transonic Flow Regime | Ansys Meshing, Ansys Fluent | Spring 2020

    I simulated the flow over the RAE2822 airfoil in the 2D domain within the transonic regime with the domain and mesh study included. I captured the delta shock accurately in terms of the location and the power. Furthermore, I presented a comparison on three RANS methods, the k-omega SST, Spalart Allmaras, and the Transition SST. Found the latter as the most accurate with the most computational cost.

    Part of the Introduction to Computational Fluid Dynamics course.

  • Two Dimensional Heat Transfer Analysis of a Laptop Chip-set | MATLAB | Spring 2019

    I numerically computed the temperature distribution within a laptop chip-set with the battery as a heat source and boundary conditions of types Dirichlet and Neumann. I analyzed the heat transfer first for a steady case and then extended the study to transient analysis.

    Part of the Heat Transfer course.
