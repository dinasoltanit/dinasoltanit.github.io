Research Experiences

  • B.Sc. Thesis, Electrical Engineering Department, Electrodynamic Analysis of Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuators Based on The Properties of Low Frequency Plasmons

    Supervisor: Dr. AmirNader Askarpour
    The purpose was to provide a mathematical model to analytically simulate plasma-based flow control actuators based on the electrodynamic considerations of the plasmonic medium. The objective is to develop the model based on fewer assumptions but more flexible to make the actuators tunable to specific modes to control the magnitude and the direction of the produced momentum vector.

  • B.Sc. Thesis, Aerospace Engineering Department, Computational Modelling of Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuators Based on The Properties of Low Frequency Plasmons

    Supervisor: Dr. Sahar Noori
    Mentor: Mr. Gholamreza Abdizadeh
    The purpose was to provide a computational model for the DBD plasma actuators, including the plasma's metamaterial properties. The model solved the governing Poisson's Equation on the considered domain, treating the plasmonic region as a dielectric media with a calculated permittivity representing the plasmonic region under excitation.

  • Investigated The Aerodynamic Characteristics Of NACA 0012, NACA 23012, NACA 4412 And SELIG 1223 Airfoils

    Supervisor: Dr. M.Tadjfar
    Mentor: Mr. Amin Jaberi
    The purpose was to provide This was thorough comparison between the numerical data from Xfoil and the experimental data from the references, regarding the aerodynamic characteristics of NACA 0012, NACA 23012, NACA 4412 and SELIG 1223 airfoils.

  • Investigated Various Numerical Solutions And Estimation Methods For Solving Blasius Equation

    Supervisor: Dr. M.Tadjfar
    Mentor: Mr. Amin Jaberi
    The purpose was to investigate the various numerical methods that are used to solve or estimate the results of the Blasius equation that represents the case of a laminar zero pressure gradient boundary layer flow on a flat plate.

Computational Analyses

  • Electrostatic Field Computation of a Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuator | MATLAB, Mathematica | Fall 2020

    I polished and programmed the method of analyzing arbitrarily oriented microstrip transmission lines in arbitrarily-shaped dielectric media. Furthermore, I computed the electrostatic fields and the capacitance matrix for the structure of a dielectric barrier discharge actuator based on the programmed method.

  • Derivation of The Closed-Form Electrostatic Green's Function for The Structure of a Dielectric Barrier Discharge Actuator | MATLAB, Mathematica | Fall 2020

    I implemented the numerical method for evaluating the highly oscillatory Bessel transform based on rewriting the Bessel function in terms of the WhittakerW function. Based on the method, I rewrote the Bessel transform as the sum of two integrals and then transformed one into the Fourier type, and computed by the generalized Gauss–Laguerre quadrature and logarithmic Gauss–Laguerre quadrature; furthermore, I computed the other integral based on the evaluation of special functions.

  • Three Dimensional Analysis of the Shock Patterns and Flow Interactions With Aerodynamic Shapes | COMSOL Multiphysics | Spring 2020

    I simulated the supersonic flow over two cone models with different shape angles and a spherical model in the 3D Domain to explore the flow field before and after the shock waves. I Collaborated with a team of 5 to conduct a visualization experiment using the Schlieren photography method to validate the numerical simulation results.

  • Numerical Analysis of The Flow over an RAE Airfoil in Transonic Flow Regime | Ansys Meshing, Ansys Fluent | Spring 2020

    I simulated the flow over the RAE2822 airfoil in the 2D domain within the transonic regime with the domain and mesh study included. I captured the delta shock accurately in terms of the location and the power. Furthermore, I presented a comparison on three RANS methods, the k-omega SST, Spalart Allmaras, and the Transition SST. Found the latter as the most accurate with the most computational cost.

  • Two Dimensional Heat Transfer Analysis of a Laptop Chip-set | MATLAB | Spring 2019

    I numerically computed the temperature distribution within a laptop chip-set with the battery as a heat source and boundary conditions of types Dirichlet and Neumann. I analyzed the heat transfer first for a steady case and then extended the study to transient analysis.

Designs and Simulations

  • Designed an Earth-to-Mars trip based on Hohmann transfer | STK, MATLAB | Spring 2021

    I simulated a trip starting from the parking orbit around the Earth, calculated using MATLAB at reasonable launch date. The trip has three main phases: 1.Escaping from the Earth orbit, 2.going through the Hohmann transfer orbit, 3.entering the Mars's orbit, and the controlling parameter was the Thrust vector of the probe.

  • Simulation of the Dynamics and The Vibration Pattern of a MEMS Sensor | MATLAB Simulink | Fall 2018

    I simulated a MEMS system, regarding its dynamics and vibrational patterns, and analyzed the characteristics of the system based on the governing equations using MATLAB Simulink.

  • Inverted Pendulum Controller Design | MATLAB Simulink | Fall 2018

    I studied and implemented different controlling strategies, Compensation, Lead-Lag, PD, PI, and PID in order to find the best senario to design a controller for an Inverted Pendulun. I simulated each designed system using MATLAB Simulink, and reported a comparison on the performance of each system with the metric of reaction times (rise time, fall time).

  • Simulator Design for a 3-degree-of-freedom Flight Simulator for Airbus-A320 | MATLAB Simulink | Fall 2018

    I implemented the flight dynamics differential equations in MATLAB Simulink. I used the simulator to study two of the widely known flight maneuvers, the cruise and the glide phases.
