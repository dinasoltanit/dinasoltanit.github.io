Autobiography

“The discovery of a complete unified theory may not aid the survival of our species, and may not even affect our life-style, but that humanity's deepest desire for knowledge is justification enough for our continuing quest, and that our goal is nothing less than a complete description of the Universe we live in.”

I remember when I first heard about M-theory. I wasn’t a Physics student. Instead, I was a double-majored student in the engineering fields, with great passions in the Physics context. Within my second and third years of university, I found myself not fully satisfied with learning. I used to enjoy the lectures and investigations. However, I used to feel locked to particular thinking ways, not being allowed to think creatively when answering questions, which seemed rationale for that field, as things had to be in the right order to be practical. To feed my curiosity, I had started reading Physics context, and the very first book I read was A Brief History of Time from Steven Hawking. His words about the unified theory have stuck with me ever since then.

Coming from a community of people who think of engineering fields as the most promising field of study for a met future life with parents who did their best to support their children from every aspect, and teachers who contributed to help me with my questions, being accepted to Amirkabir University was a significant event. For my family and me, this was a way to ensure that I would have the chance to leave behind the struggles that they have grown up with. Attributing my success to the community of people who have supported me, I have been privileged to receive a world-class education in a field which, from my point of view, provided me with this understanding that human beings are able to think of both abstract and concrete subjects that are far away from the daily ones.

When I was about to finish my second year at the university, I managed to pursue another degree in Electrical Engineering. Based on results from consultations with alumni and my instructors, I thought this combination of studies would provide me with a more thorough background in Physics. At the same time, I had experienced enjoying night walks, coming back from my late-night studies at the university’s library, and listening to The Grand Design, The First Three Minutes, and similar topics. Through these transformative experiences, I realized that where I wanted to continue my studies was to provide a better complete description of the universe. However, the unanswered question for me was how I can help this quest. How can I use my education to add value to this field? I found the answer when I was listening to Katie Bouman’s TED Talks video. She talked about her challenging responsibility, putting the data from different telescopes in a pattern to provide the first image of a black hole without being biased to a possible prediction. I got the idea of where I needed to be and where I could make the most significant impact. There are both theoretical and experimental explorations with a united purpose of providing a more in-depth explanation for different aspects of the universe. In this regard, I want to put the skills I have gained into practice, make more precise predictions to provide theories and gather data with a frame from which more detailed approximations and projections would be comprehended.

I have chosen to have my education centered on computation, with a foundation in research and innovation. Therefore, I am actively looking forward to find a graduate position which would give me what I need to become a professional. After developing my skills further, I plan to pursue an academic career in applied physics, and practice my passion for the universe.
